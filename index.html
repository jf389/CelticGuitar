History of Celtic Music

Celtic Music is folk music that originates from the Celtic countries of Ireland, Scotland and Wales. The inhabatants of these countries (The Celts) created a distiinctive musical voice, which is what gives this genere of music its name today.

Celtic Music can be traced back to the early 1600's and still to this day is a popular genere of music. Common instruments you might notice in Celtic music include violin, lute, flute, bagpipes, harp, and today the guitar.

Irish Traditional Music

The time signatures and styles of tunes commonly found in Irish music include single jig (12/8 time), double jig (6/8 time), reel (4/4 time), hornpipe (swing 4/4 time), slip jig (9/8 time), and occasionally versions of polkas (2/4 time) and mazurkas or waltzes (3/4 time). All of these tune styles have corresponding traditional dances.

Scottish Traditional Music

The time signatures and styles of tunes commonly found in Scottish music include waltz (3/4 time), march (4/4 time), reel (4/4 time), stratthspy (scotch-snap 4/4 time), jig (6/8 or 9/8 time), and slow airs (6/8 time). All of these tune styles also have corresponding traditional dances.

Fingerstyle Techniques

Sitting

In classical guitar, the guitar sits on your weak sided leg tilted up at a 45 degree angle. It is important to be sitting towards the edge of your chair with your back completely perpindicular to the chair. Your arm may rest on the crest of the guitar's face but not part of your hand should touch the face. Now using your thumb (mainly to pick the top three strings) and you index, middle, and ring fingers (to pick the lower three strings) You can pick the guitar remembering not to rest the palm of your hand on the face of the guitar.

Nail Shape

Although it is not neccesary to do so, growing your nails and shapping them like the example shown above, can ressult in clarity and better tonality. When shapping your nails make sure that your index, middle and ring fingernails are gently slopped toward the pinkie. Your thumb should be sloped in the oposite direction. The reason for slopping the fingernails and not letting them grow straight, is due to the way a guitarist whill strike the strings. The string will hit the shallower part of the nail and glide up the slop to give an even tone across striking the strings.

Learn to Read

Learning to read sheet music is a good way to be able to learn any of the Celtic music arrangments online. As there is not really a lot of videos with teachers to direct you through a song, learning to read will give you the advantage of being able to pick up any sheet of music and perform the song imediately.
